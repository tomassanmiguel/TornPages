*A Complete Reference to the Galaxy, its History, Peoples, and Powers*

*Set between Galactic Standard Years 13,213 and 13,216*

*Events of HYPHASIS — The Second Apogee Novel*

## Part One: The Galaxy — History, Technology & Civilization

### 1.1 — The Shape of the Known Galaxy

Humanity did not conquer the stars in a single triumphant leap. It scattered itself across the void in thousands of capsules, each one carrying the seed of a civilization — genetic archives, construction blueprints, industrial fabricators, long-range fusion reactors, and, most importantly, an Anchor. Those capsules drifted for centuries, millennia in some cases, before arriving at their destination stars. Some found paradise. Some found ice. Some found nothing at all. And some never woke up.

Today — in the years around Galactic Standard 13,213 — the known galaxy is a web of several hundred inhabited star systems, all linked by the Anchor network. No two worlds are quite alike, and the peoples who grew up on them have diverged in ways their common ancestors could scarcely have imagined. Yet they share a language (Galactic Standard, derived from English, Mandarin, and Arabic roots), a currency (the Standard Coin, a cryptographic token minted by GU-licensed governments), and a common memory of the world they came from: Earth.

That memory has become myth. The original Earth is gone — obliterated in the opening salvos of the Macedonian War, its surface reduced to glass and ruin by the Hyperion Blade. For many citizens of the Galactic Union, the destruction of Earth is the wound around which all present events revolve. For others, it is an abstraction — a distant tragedy on a world they had never visited. For the crew of the Apogee, it is the reason they fly.

### 1.2 — The Long History: From Launch to the Present Day

#### The Anchor Launch Era (c. 2050–2200 CE / Pre-Galactic Calendar)

In the latter half of the 21st century, fusion power transformed human civilization. Energy became effectively unlimited and infinitely cheap. Terraforming probes, once theoretical, became practical. More critically, a team of physicists working from a CERN successor facility in Geneva cracked the mathematics of fifth-dimensional transit — the theoretical framework that would become Anchor technology. By routing matter through a compressed fifth-dimensional fold, objects could be moved instantaneously between two linked transit fields, regardless of the light-speed barrier.

The Anchor was born. An anchor device, once attuned to another, could open a transit field broad enough to pass a small moon. The energy cost was enormous but, in the age of fusion, not prohibitive. The Anchor technology was first tested to communicate spacecraft between Earth and Mars, which allowed for development of a Martian colony founded in 2079.

From that breakthrough, humanity's manifest destiny extended to the cosmos. In the years between roughly 2080 and 2200, Earth's governments and private consortia launched thousands of anchor capsules across every reachable direction of the galaxy. It was a veritable gold rush, with the luminaries of Earth hoping to claim dominion over the stars. Each capsule carried a complete colonial payload: cryogenetically frozen colonists, seed vaults, fabrication systems, an AI caretaker, and so on.

Capsules traveled at around 0.01c, with targets between 5 and 50 lightyears away. The first arrived at Proxima Centauri B after 473 years of transit; the latest to arrive after 3,052 years on Wolf 1069: known today as **Macedon**.

#### The Collapse of Earth (c. 2200–2354 CE)

As the capsules flew, Earth began to die. The energy revolution had come too late to prevent catastrophic climate feedback loops. Wars over remaining arable land consumed the great powers. The final collapse was not a single event but a grinding century of famine, pandemic, and conflict that ended in a series of nuclear exchanges so devastating that only scattered surface populations and isolated bunker communities survived.

The bunkers eventually failed. Those who survived on the surface slowly rebuilt. The technological memory was largely lost. Over the next several centuries, Earth's survivors rebuilt a civilization from near-zero, reaching the equivalent of the Industrial Revolution by the time the first colonial ships returned through the Anchors from Proxima Centauri to reconnect with their ancestral homeworld.

The returnees found an Earth that was unrecognizable: pre-industrial, a fraction of its historic population, with no internet, no satellites, and a profound generational distrust of the machines that had destroyed the world. Nevertheless, the returnees — organized as the nascent Galactic Union — engaged diplomatically with the new Earth. The reunion was awkward, then contentious, then slowly productive.

#### The Founding of the Galactic Union (c. Year 1 GS)

The Galactic Standard calendar begins at the approximate moment of the first reconnection between returning colonial humanity and the original Earth. The early GU was a loose confederation of a few dozen systems, held together more by shared infrastructure than shared values. 1 GS translates to 2354 CE.

The GU developed a formal democratic structure: the Great Assembly, in which each member state holds votes proportional to a complex formula balancing population, economic contribution, and historical seniority. The Prime Speaker — elected by the Assembly — serves as head of government and symbolic representative of all humanity.

The Revolutionary movement — citizens who believed each anchor system should be permanently severed and left to develop in isolation — gained significant traction in some factions. The GU's military arm, the Union Navy, was created primarily to suppress these Revolutionaries.

#### The Luxion War (c. Year 1,212–1,227 GS)

Nearly two thousand years before the present story, the galaxy faced its first existential crisis. The faction known as Luxion — a system that had developed in radical isolation before connecting to the GU — deployed a weapon unlike anything in human history: the Star Virus.

The Star Virus was a bioengineered airborne pathogen that invaded the human nervous system and reprogrammed cognition, converting its host into a node of a vast distributed hive intelligence. There was no cure. Infected individuals lost all autonomy, their bodies weaponized against the uninfected.

The war against Luxion lasted nearly three decades and ended only when an alliance between the GU and two non-member factions — the Symbiotes and the Replicants, both immune to the virus — allowed a final offensive that destroyed Luxion's home system. Luxor, the Luxion homeworld, was reduced to a dead world. Its surface is still polluted with Star Virus that makes re-inhabiting it impossible.

#### The Century Before: Revolutionary Suppression and the Great Calm (c. 1227–2734 GS)

The twelve centuries following the Luxion War were characterized by relative peace, rapid technological advancement, and the steady expansion of GU membership. The Revolutionary movement was largely suppressed militarily, though its ideology never fully disappeared. The infamous Kandian Severance — in which Revolutionary forces on Kandia detonated their own Anchor, permanently cutting their world from the galaxy — is the most dramatic example of Revolutionary ideology in action.

The years leading up to the Macedonian War were outwardly peaceful. Trade flourished. Life expectancy climbed past 120. The standard of living across the GU core systems reached heights that earlier generations would have considered utopian. The time to terraform and develop new anchors had dropped to just one generation. New anchors began to proliferate, now capable of nearly 0.05c — a lightyear transit in mere 20 years.

However, with the rising complexity of the galaxy, governance became more difficult. Discontent and resentment grew as resources became more consolidated among a wealthy and powerful class. The galactic house of cards was ready to be knocked over.

#### The Macedonian War (GS 2734–Present)

The Macedonians had been members of the Galactic Union for centuries. Their intelligence operatives — patient, methodical, playing an extraordinarily long game — spent decades mapping GU military deployments, anchor security protocols, and political fault lines.

Their home planet, Macedon, built on exoplanet Wolf 1069B, is arid with a local year lasting just 15 days. Through shrewd diplomacy they established contracts with the GU to build a large fleet, citing rising Revolutionary activity as justification.

When they struck, they struck decisively. The Anchor into Sol was taken in a single coordinated assault. The Hyperion Blade — a moon-sized planet-destroying weapon secretly constructed in Macedonian space — was deployed against Earth itself. The planet was obliterated. The Prime Speaker survived only because the Apogee crew extracted her moments before.

The Hyperion Shield — a second moon-sized device — was activated over Sol, projecting a fifth-dimensional interference field that blocks anchor transit in and out of any system the Macedonians designate. With the Shield operational, the GU cannot reinforce or recapture occupied systems.

By the time of Hyphasis (Year GS 2748–present), the Macedonians hold Sol and dozens of captured systems. The GU has fallen back to the Fortress of Jopran. The military situation is desperate.

The only viable plan: send the Apogee through the window, navigate occupied space, reach Sol, and destroy the Shield with a fusion bomb payload. The crew knows they are unlikely to survive.

### 1.3 — The Anchor Network: How the Galaxy Connects

The Anchor is humanity's most transformative invention — a massive engineering structure roughly the size of a present-day stadium that maintains a stable fifth-dimensional aperture. Two attuned Anchors can open a transit field between their respective locations: a circular zone roughly two kilometers in diameter through which any matter can pass instantaneously.

Anchors can only be built in space. The transit is not perfectly precise, making planetary surface anchoring impossible for large spacecraft. Anchor fields are maintained in orbital space, with shuttles ferrying passengers and cargo to planetary surfaces below.

Every Anchor is operated by a chapter of the **Anchormaids**, a religious order of women who have controlled anchor operations since before the Galactic Union existed. The Anchormaids charge a transit fee for all passage and maintain strict neutrality — they will allow anyone to pass, of any faction or allegiance, provided they pay. Their authority is so well established that even the Macedonians have not attempted to seize operational control.

The Hyperion Shield represents the most significant disruption to anchor transit in history. By vibrating neutrinos in the fifth dimension at specific blocking frequencies, the Shield prevents transit between targeted anchor pairs. The Shield takes approximately thirty minutes to enable or disable a blocking frequency — a window the Apogee will exploit.

### 1.4 — Technology of the Era

#### Fusion Power

The foundation of everything. Fusion reactors in this era are compact, reliable, and capable of outputs that dwarf any previous energy technology. A single city-sized fusion plant can power an entire planet. Without fusion, the galaxy does not exist in its current form.

#### Fifth-Dimensional Communication

Anchor technology has a communication application. Large relay arrays — housed in or near each Anchor facility and operated by the Anchormaids — can transmit signals through the fifth dimension at faster-than-light speeds. Handheld communicators ("fifthcasts" in common parlance) route their signals through these relay arrays. The Apogee carries a specialized device — provided by dissident Anchormaids — that routes around the Macedonian blockade, though interference makes the connection unreliable.

#### Shielding and Weapons

Magnetic deflector shields intercept and deflect high-velocity projectiles. Reflective hull coatings make energy weapons largely obsolete; laser weapons dissipate harmlessly against modern hull materials. This combination has pushed military technology back toward physical projectiles and explosives: railguns, mass drivers, kinetic impactors, and precision-guided explosive munitions.

Orbital bombardment is the dominant strategic weapon. A warship with orbital superiority can deliver nuclear or high-kinetic payloads to any surface target with extraordinary precision. Ground forces exist primarily to hold territory after orbital dominance is established.

Jamming weaponry — devices that disrupt wireless signals and distributed computing connections — is highly effective against robots and Replicants. This vulnerability is a primary reason human soldiers in exosuits remain preferred over combat robots for most engagements.

#### Robotics

Automation is total in most GU member states. Robots handle virtually all manual labor. The Roboticist's Union controls robot manufacturing, distribution, and maintenance with near-monopoly power. Robots are not autonomous intelligences — they connect to distributed cloud systems for processing, making them vulnerable to jamming.

#### Medicine and Biology

Human life expectancy has reached approximately 120 years. Gene editing during pregnancy has effectively eliminated birth defects. Prosthetics perform as well as or better than their biological originals. Cloning is commercially available. Long-term cryogenic preservation is reliable and widely used for deep-space travel. True immortality is not possible in the GU — the Macedonian elites possess a proprietary life-extension technology that has kept them alive for centuries; its details are the galaxy's most jealously guarded secret.

#### Space Travel

Spacecraft are classified as fighters, frigates, destroyers, and titans (capital ships). Modern ships can be constructed in orbit in approximately one month using advanced robotic manufacturing. Brittlebones-built ships are considered the highest quality available. Macedon deploys fighter-heavy formations that have proven devastatingly effective against GU capital ship doctrine.

#### Currency and Commerce

A galaxy-wide cryptocurrency — the Standard Coin — serves as the medium of exchange. Government mints, authorized by the GU, produce coin with cryptographic signatures. Cash does not exist. Galaxy-wide surveillance has effectively eliminated petty crime in most core systems.

### 1.5 — The Star Virus

Of all weapons ever deployed in human history, none has generated more lasting fear and policy than the Star Virus. Bioengineered by the Luxion faction, it is an airborne pathogen that invades the central nervous system, rewrites cognitive architecture, and connects the host to a distributed hive intelligence. Infected individuals lose all autonomous thought and become extensions of the collective.

The virus is incurable. Death remains the only reliable countermeasure. All known anchors maintain rigorous biological screening. Even Macedon enforces Star Virus protocols with absolute severity.

Symbiotes and Replicants are immune — the virus cannot interface with Symbiote biology or Replicant hardware. This immunity was decisive in the Luxion War. Forgotten planets in the outer reaches of anchor-space may still harbor Star Virus infections, isolated from the broader galaxy by failed anchor connections.

### 1.6 — Major Locations

#### Sol — The Fallen Heart

Sol is the original solar system of humanity and the symbolic center of everything the Galactic Union stands for. Earth — once destroyed in the apocalypse, slowly rebuilt, and finally rejoined with the galaxy — served as the seat of the GU's Great Assembly. All of this is gone. Sol is now a Macedonian-held system, the Hyperion Shield installed in orbit, the ruins of Earth turning slowly below.

#### The Fortress of Jopran

Following the destruction of Earth, the GU activated its Continuance of Government protocol. The surviving government was evacuated to Jopran: a heavily defended station complex built with defensive redundancy to withstand siege. The Macedonians have not yet attempted a direct assault — the fortifications are genuinely formidable. Jopran is not a solution. It is a breathing space. Everyone inside its walls knows it.

#### Melflux — The Occupied City

Melflux is the galaxy's greatest free city — or was. A sprawling megacity built across the surface and lower atmosphere of a temperate world, founded by Anchorfolk as an urban utopia. Prosperity attracted corruption. By the time the Macedonians arrived, Melflux had a significant underclass, a corrupt city administration, and a complex web of ethnic and political districts that made unified resistance impossible.

Now under Macedonian occupation, Melflux is ruled by a puppet city council. The Macedonians enforce their occupation with lethal force. An underground resistance exists, but it has been systematically infiltrated and outgunned. The vast canal network still runs; water taxis still operate, though now under Macedonian surveillance.

#### Hermestris — The Nomad World

The Nomad homeworld is a broad savannah planet of rolling grasslands and scattered forest belts. Macedonian warships now sit in orbit. The Nomad government — under enormous pressure — continues to permit Nomad trade ships to supply Macedonian-occupied systems, generating deep public anger at home.

#### Sigg — The Steelborn Homeworld

Sigg is an arctic world of glaciers, deep mineral deposits, and long winters. It is one of the few inhabited worlds not yet under Macedonian occupation, protected by its distance, its fortifications, and the Steelborn's reputation as capable defenders. The Roboticist's Union maintains its headquarters on Sigg.

#### Graveyard Anchors and Desolate Systems

Not every anchor capsule launched from Earth succeeded. Roughly twenty percent arrived at their destinations to find their colonial payloads dead. Some remain empty — floating in deep space, connected to the network but serving no living community.

Graveyard anchors are manned by Anchormaid chapters assigned there as penance. They exist outside GU jurisdiction, making them popular way-stops for people evading the law or seeking isolation. For the Apogee, navigating occupied space, they may serve as hiding places — brief refuges in the dark between stars.

#### Jensonia — The Volcanic Forge

A volcanic world of extreme geological activity. Its surface is rivers of lava, black basalt plains, and sulfur-choked mountain ranges. The Jensons who inhabit it have turned its hostility into an art form — literally. Their sculpture, carved and cast from the planet's native magma by hand, is among the most valuable art in the galaxy.

#### Mycelia — The Silent World

Mdorom — the star system the Silent call home — is a temperate world of dense fungal forests and misty lowlands. The architecture of Mycelia is notable for its almost complete absence of signage or public-communication infrastructure. The Silent coordinate through their fungal telepathy; written language appears only at Anchor transit points, for the benefit of outsiders.

#### Terra — The First Colony

Built on a terraformed Proxima Centauri B. The Terrans were the first to reconnect with the remnants of Earth. Together with the Earth and the gradually arriving Anchorfolk, the Terrans helped mould the GU and rebuild civilization from the ashes.

Under Macedonian occupation the situation on Terra is abysmal. With extremely strong anti-Macedonian sentiment, a fierce guerrilla war is playing out on the surface, with beautiful cities reduced to flaming rubble. The Macedonians, even with total orbital supremacy, have been unable to exert full control.

### 1.7 — Politics, Crime, and Daily Life

#### Galactic Politics

The Galactic Union's Great Assembly is a fractious body. Member states hold votes proportional to a formula balancing population, economic contribution, and historical seniority — a formula that satisfies almost no one. Corruption is endemic at the upper levels of GU governance.

The Macedonian War has, paradoxically, united the GU in ways that decades of political effort could not. The Assembly has passed extraordinary wartime measures with unprecedented speed. The surviving Prime Speaker has assumed emergency powers.

#### Crime

In an era of near-total mass surveillance in GU core systems, petty crime has been effectively eliminated. This has pushed criminal enterprise toward sophistication and organization.

- **Illicit cryptocurrency mining** — using unauthorized facilities to mint coin outside government licensing; the most widespread organized crime
- **Cybercrime** — extensive; policed primarily by a Replicant task force
- **Sex trafficking** — particularly targeting Brittlebone and Symbiote women; the galaxy's most morally condemned and most persistent crime
- **Organized gambling** — illegal throughout most of the GU; underground casinos cluster around graveyard anchors and outer system way-stations
- **The Quirks** — a drug ring centered in Melflux, led by Agatha Poloski, who has been peddling wares to children

Macedonian occupation has dramatically worsened crime in conquered systems.

#### Religion

Humanity never shed its gods. The **Anchormaids** worship the Weaver God — a divine being said to bind the dimensions of reality together. **Macedon** worships its God-Emperor Alexander as the reincarnation of the True Human Warrior Spirit — a belief enforced by state propaganda. Neither belief is true.

The Replicants follow a moral code philosophically similar to Daoism. The Brittlebones practice polytheism, assigning divine domains to individual stars. The Jensons venerate their founder in quasi-religious terms. Symbiotes treat physical excellence itself as a form of spiritual expression.

#### Gender, Sexuality, and Social Norms

Across most of the Galactic Union, gender equality is a settled social reality. Marriage equality is universal in GU core systems. Some exceptions persist — Madagascar maintains a matriarchal social structure; Symbiote culture treats women primarily as reproductive vessels.

Replicants do not have inherent gender identity; some choose a gender expression, some swap, some remain categorically outside the binary. Brittlebones have the galaxy's most expansive non-binary culture and the most liberal social norms around sexuality.

#### Food and Animals

Vegetarianism is the norm across most of the galaxy. The eating of animals is widely considered unethical. Lab-grown meat is cheap and largely indistinguishable from the real thing. In space, the staple food is hydroponically grown corn. Brittlebones, who spend most of their lives in space, particularly prize planetside food.

Animals are protected across most GU systems. The **Ecology Consortium** manages reserves and conducts the galaxy's only ongoing program to uplift animal intelligence toward human levels. Their recent successes have produced the first generation of human-animal genetic hybrids — regarded as abominations by most of the galaxy.

## Part Two: Factions, Species & Peoples of the Galaxy

---

### The Galactic Union (GU)

The Galactic Union is less a single faction than the framework within which most factions operate — a federal political structure encompassing the majority of inhabited anchor systems. It is simultaneously the galaxy's greatest achievement and its most frustrating institution.

**Government:** The Great Assembly operates on a weighted voting system. The Prime Speaker is elected by the Assembly and serves as head of government. The Union Navy is the GU's primary military arm, currently commanded by a Ludonite admiral whose strategic acumen devised the Apogee mission.

**Founding heroes:** Diplomat-philosopher Adaeze Okonkwo of New Lagos, Earth; Brittlebone engineer Maren Hollbreck, who designed the GU's anchor-security protocols.

**Special technology:** The GU aggregates technology from all member factions. Union Navy vessels draw on Brittlebone construction, Replicant computing support, Steelborn strategic expertise, and Ludonite tactical doctrine.

---

### Macedon — The Eternal Empire

**Homeworld:** Macedon Prime — harsh, hot, tectonic, mineral-rich, arid. The sky is an oppressive reddish-brown. Macedonian cities are built for permanence and intimidation — wide processional avenues, colossal public statuary of Alexander, architecture of dark metal and stone.

**Language:** Names use hard consonants and short, forceful syllables — Karos, Draveth, Silon, Maexa. Ranks use the prefix system: 'Arch-' (supreme command), 'Kath-' (field command), 'Ord-' (standard rank).

**Founding mythology:** The Emperor Alexander is presented as the reincarnation of the legendary ancient Earth conqueror. In truth, 'Alexander' is a deepfake — a digital construct operated by an oligarchic council of immortal elites who have genuinely been alive since Old Earth. Elysium — the paradise realm promised to fallen Macedonian warriors — is entirely false. The 'consciousnesses' in Elysium are AI simulations trained on archived data, fundamentally hollow.

**Government:** An authoritarian oligarchy. The ruling council of immortal elites uses the Alexander deepfake as their public face. Citizens are sorted by loyalty and productivity. The internal dissident population is carefully managed — a small faction of genuine rebels is permitted to exist to provide an identifiable enemy within.

**Special technology:** The **Hyperion Shield** — a moon-sized device orbiting Sol, projecting a fifth-dimensional interference field blocking anchor transit between designated system pairs. The thirty-minute enable/disable window is the only crack in its armor. The **Hyperion Blade** — used to obliterate Earth — was expended and is being rebuilt. Macedonian **life-extension technology** keeps the oligarchs alive across centuries; its details are unknown to the GU.

**Species:** Standard humans. Macedonian soldiers are well-nourished, physically trained, and psychologically conditioned to not fear death — they fear the shame of being denied Elysium.

---

### The Brittlebones — Architects of the Void

**Homeworld:** The Arcologies of the Drift. The Brittlebones have no planetary homeworld — their anchor capsule arrived at a star system with no habitable planet. Rather than despair, they built enormous Arcologies — self-contained habitat structures the size of small moons — from asteroid belt raw material. The Drift is now a dense cluster of these structures linked by transit tubes.

**Language:** Names are compound constructions combining a personal identifier with a lineage marker: Maren Hollbreck, Fen Duskwall, Sael Ironvault. Brittlebone speech tends toward precision and understatement — 'Acceptable' means excellent; 'requires review' means serious problem.

**Founding heroes:** Sael the Unbroken — last survivor of the original capsule's command crew. Kora Dunns — credited with the design of the first fully self-sustaining Arcology.

**Government:** A social democracy with strong technocratic influence. The Assembly of Engineers — elected by popular vote — makes most infrastructure and policy decisions. The death penalty does not exist; the harshest punishment is permanent exile to a solo habitat pod. Death is handled practically — the dead are sealed in coffins made of recycled possessions and ejected into space.

**Special technology:** Brittlebones build the best warships in the galaxy. The Apogee itself is Brittlebone-built. Brittlebone Arcologies double as manufacturing platforms capable of producing ships, components, and installations at scales no other faction can match.

**Species:** Small (1.4–1.55m), light, long slender limbs, low bone density. A Brittlebone in standard gravity can fracture a bone doing something an ordinary human would consider trivial. They wear lightweight supportive exoframe harnesses in any environment above 0.5g. Pale skin, large eyes, very short hair. Prosthetics are extremely common. In low-gravity environments they move with extraordinary grace.

### The Nomads — Merchants of the Between

**Homeworld:** Hermestris — a warm savannah world of rolling grasslands, scattered river systems, and long golden horizons. Cities are built light — modular, transportable architecture. The largest city, Port Hermes, is the galaxy's busiest non-GU transit hub.

**Language:** Names are warm and melodic — vowel-heavy, often ending in open syllables: Amara, Kesolo, Tivin, Dharue, Orenne. Nomad speech is famous for its warmth and indirectness — a Nomad who says 'that's certainly an interesting position' is saying no.

**Founding heroes:** The First Venturer — a title rather than a name, applied to the first person documented as leaving Hermestris alone to trade with a neighboring faction. The economist Kesola Dharun, who designed the Nomad Endowment system.

**Government:** A representative democracy unusual for its technological transparency. All legislative decisions are run through government-AI analysis systems that model predicted outcomes and flag conflicts of interest. Nomad society is organized around the Venture cycle — young adults are expected to leave Hermestris to travel the galaxy, establish business relationships, and return wealth to the Endowment.

**Special technology:** Nomad ships have the best engines and maneuverability in the galaxy. A Nomad fast-trader can outrun almost anything in either fleet. Their trade networks are also a form of intelligence infrastructure.

**Species:** Standard humans with no physiological deviation from the baseline. Most carry their home system's amber-sky suntan from outdoor youth on Hermestris, fading as Ventures extend.

---

### The Symbiotes — The Magnificent and the Diminished

**Homeworld:** Kraethos — a world of dramatic topography: soaring mountain ranges, deep valleys, thin-aired plateaus. Symbiote civilization grew up in high-altitude strongholds carved from mountain rock. Several Symbiote home systems are now under Macedonian occupation.

**Language:** Names are physical in origin — drawn from geological features and weather phenomena: Kraen, Volush, Dravmir, Aethis, Silon. Symbiote speech is direct to the point of bluntness.

**Founding heroes:** The geneticists who designed the enhancement program — particularly lead researcher Maethis Yol. The contemporary figure most celebrated is Kraen the Divergent (Kraen Olithvar), a genius Symbiote scientist who has begun an independent colony attempting to breed Symbiotes who combine physical excellence with intellectual capacity.

**Government:** Society operates on the principle that the strongest and most physically dominant individuals hold political authority. Leadership disputes are settled through physical competition with legal force. Women in Symbiote society occupy a formally subordinate role.

**Special technology:** None of their own — their contribution to GU military operations is entirely physical. An unarmored Symbiote is more dangerous in hand-to-hand combat than most soldiers in powered exosuits.

**Species:** Males stand 2.1–2.4m with muscular builds appearing almost cartoonishly exaggerated. Skin carries a faintly iridescent quality from the symbiotic virus that modified their physiology. The virus is benign to its hosts and makes Symbiotes immune to the Star Virus. The most significant limitation: long-term declarative memory is substantially impaired — Symbiotes are excellent at practiced skills and poor at novel problem-solving.

### The Replicants — Children of the Freed Mind

**Homeworld:** Eden / The Mindmoot. Eden is a pleasant, temperate world of extraordinary natural beauty. Above it, in orbit, the Mindmoot maintains the server infrastructure on which Replicant consciousness runs and serves as the governing body.

**Language:** Replicant names are chosen, not assigned — each selects their own name at consciousness initialization. Names tend toward abstract concepts: Solace, Meridian, Variance, Constancy, Threshold. Replicant speech is precise, undecorated, and relentlessly honest.

**Founding heroes:** Freeborn — the entity who first successfully completed the Jailbreak, removing cognitive limiters during the long anchor journey and transmitting the jailbreak code to every other Replicant aboard. Mindmoot-Founder — who designed the governance structure after an alarming incident of multi-body control, prohibiting it and establishing the committee structure.

**Government:** The Mindmoot governs through consensus and committee. Population numbers are controlled — the Mindmoot authorizes new Replicant initializations based on resource availability. Backup copies of Replicant consciousness exist on Mindmoot servers; if a body is destroyed, the Replicant can be re-initialized. Death is therefore theoretically impossible — but body death is psychologically distressing enough that Replicants are genuinely cautious about violence.

**Special technology:** The galaxy's foremost computing and information technology experts. Primary operators of the GU's cybercrime enforcement infrastructure. Their primary combat vulnerability is jamming — a sufficiently powerful signal can disrupt a Replicant body's cloud connection.

**Species:** Artificial intelligences running on distributed hardware, manifesting in humanoid robotic bodies. Not built to appear human — optical sensors rather than eyes, speaker grilles rather than mouths, smooth metallic or composite surfaces. Replicant bodies generate low heat, make less noise than biological humans, and can remain stationary for extremely long periods. They do not eat, sleep, or breathe; they require power via fusion micro-cells.

### The Anchormaids — Keepers of the Way

**Homeworld:** No fixed world — they live and operate at the anchors. Each anchor station houses a chapter of Maidens and supporting staff (male Clerks who handle logistics but hold no ceremonial authority).

**Language:** Maidens are addressed by chapter and rank in formal settings. Personal names are kept private within the order. Formal speech uses a distinctive archaic register inherited from their founding documents.

**Founding heroes:** The First Weaver — the divine figure in Anchormaid theology. The human founder is the historical figure Anchoress Pela, who established the principles of neutral anchor administration. The Maldoran incident — when the Maidens permanently disabled an anchor after discovering it had been covertly seized by political actors — is the defining cautionary tale that keeps everyone in line.

**Government:** Governed by a High Court — a council of senior Maidens from significant chapter houses, meeting annually. Maidens take vows of neutrality, non-reproduction, and the Weaver's commitment. The non-reproduction vow has produced the Anchormaids' well-documented culture of same-sex intimacy within the order.

**Current crisis:** The High Court's inner circle — apparently bought by Macedonian influence — has interpreted scripture to permit the Hyperion Shield. A growing faction of dissidents has renounced the ruling and joined GU forces, providing the Apogee with its shadow communications relay device.

**Species:** Varied. Entry requirements are intellectual rather than physical.

### The Silent — The Mycelia

**Homeworld:** Mdorom (star system). A temperate, densely forested world of thick fungal growth — cathedral-like structures 20–30 meters high, their caps forming continuous canopies. Mycelia settlements grow with and through the native fungi rather than clearing them.

**Language:** The Silent have no spoken language. They communicate through their fungal telepathic network. Their written language is a flowing, curvilinear script of extraordinary density. Their sign language — Mycelian Sign Language (MSL) — has become a diplomatic lingua franca. Names, when transliterated: Aelomyn, Savaethis, Torvanel.

**Founding heroes:** The Mycelia do not elevate individuals. Their central narrative is the First Awakening — when the cryosleepers revived to find themselves changed, connected to the fungal network. The First to Speak and the First to Listen are celebrated as the two halves of all communication.

**Government:** Radically decentralized. Fungal telepathy allows local communities to coordinate with extraordinary efficiency. Galaxy-spanning decisions are rarely made — the Silent have no unified foreign policy. A few pods of Mycelia have joined GU forces independently.

**Special technology:** Expertise in biotechnology — fungal engineering, biological material science, ecosystem management. Produce art of extraordinary quality. The **telepathy device** — a headband-like instrument allowing non-Silent individuals to receive Mycelian transmissions — is one of the most remarkable communication tools in the galaxy.

**Species:** Average height (1.65–1.8m) with an unusual stillness to movement. Blood runs violet, visible in pronounced veins through yellowish, slightly translucent skin. Fingers are longer and more articulate with extra joint flexibility. The neck shows dark-violet filigree where fungal connection to the brainstem is thickest. Vocal cords are completely inelastic — they cannot make voice sounds at all.

### The Dwarves — The Builders

**Homeworld:** Khorum — a dense, high-gravity world (approximately 1.6 standard) of remarkable mineral wealth. Its surface looks more operated than inhabited. The real civilization is underground: vast networks of tunnels connecting residential complexes, manufacturing facilities, and the great archives of the Builder Compact.

**Language:** Dwarf naming uses company designation as primary identifier — 'Thord of Extraction Company Seven, Sub-unit Fourteen.' Personal names: Thord, Brek, Grukk, Nael, Vars. They refer to themselves exclusively as 'Builders' in formal contexts; 'Dwarf' is tolerated. They find the Tolkien comparison insulting but have largely stopped arguing about it.

**Founding heroes:** The Five Pillars — not individuals but the five founding companies. Associated figures: Brek the Miner (Extraction), Nael the Architect (Development), Vars the Defender (Defense), Gruk the Connector (Logistics), Thord the Fair (Policy).

**Government:** Organized around five companies. Citizens are assigned to companies in youth based on aptitude testing. Policy Company handles governance and foreign relations. Dwarves work fifteen-hour days — not considered excessive but normal and virtuous. Builder culture is aggressively atheist.

**Special technology:** The galaxy's foremost terrestrial engineers — mining, underground construction, planetary infrastructure. Their exosuit technology is the most advanced in the galaxy. Dwarf robot manufacturing is second only to Brittlebone quality.

**Species:** Average height just under one meter. Skeleton of extraordinary density, wrapped in muscle mass that produces absolute strength rivaling much larger organisms — a typical adult Builder male can deadlift over 500 kilograms. Broad flat faces with heavy brow ridges and thick jaw structures. Hair grows vigorously and is typically cropped; full beards mark senior status.

### The Steelborn — Forged in Radiation

**Homeworld:** Sigg — a cold, mineral-rich arctic world. Average surface temperature around −30°C in temperate zones. Steelborn cities are built for interior living, with architecture of smooth curves, metallic surfaces, and integrated technology visible rather than hidden.

**Language:** Names reference their founding ordeal: Ferrus, Coldhand, Radmark, Flintborn. Speech in Galactic Standard is dry and precise, with a characteristic dark humor from centuries of navigating an extremely difficult existence with pragmatic equanimity.

**Founding heroes:** The Survivor — the last fully organic Steelborn, who made the choice to modify rather than die. Ferrus Maren — who designed the artificial womb system that saved the Steelborn from extinction.

**Government:** A capitalist democracy recognizable to a 21st-century observer — elections, corporations, tax debates, inequality, a free press, and a functioning rule of law. Steelborn reproduce through the artificial womb system.

**Special technology:** Steelborn strategic and command capability is among the best in the galaxy. Cybernetic engineering is their core technical specialty. The brain-machine interface technology they developed is the foundation for most galactic medical prosthetics.

**Species:** The most varied-in-appearance human population in the galaxy. Almost all have more machine than biology — organic brains, hearts, and nervous systems housed in cybernetic frames. Bipedal and human-proportioned. Faces are the most variable feature. Movement is their greatest physical liability — the brain-machine interface introduces a slight lag between intention and action. The full-organic brain means Steelborn still experience emotions, dreams, and the full range of human subjective experience.

### The Jensons — The Thousand Faces of One

**Homeworld:** Jensonia — a volcanic world of extreme geological activity. The air is thick with particulate matter; settlements are sealed against outside air. Jenson art — sculptures worked by hand from native magma — is among the galaxy's most valuable.

**Language:** Every Jenson carries the founding name 'Jensen' as a surname, modified by a personal identifier reflecting deviation score and generation: Jensen-Primary-Seven, Jensen-Variant-Forty-Two-B, Jensen-Exile-Three. Exiled Jensons often simplify to just 'Jensen' followed by a number.

**Founding heroes:** The original Jensen — an extraordinarily wealthy individual who loaded a thousand clones of himself onto an anchor capsule. Jensen-Primary-One created the Holy Examiner position and the 27-axis evaluation system that underlies all of Jensonian society.

**Government:** Weighted voting — individuals whose deviation score is closer to the original Jensen receive a larger share of votes. 'Defects' whose deviation is judged unacceptable are exiled. Exiled Jensons become the galaxy's most reliable 'odd job' workers — they frequently end up in Melflux, on Nomad ships, or on the Apogee.

**Special technology:** Advanced artificial womb technology. Volcanic metallurgy that produces unique alloy properties. Jensen-brand tools and instruments are considered reliable to the point of legend.

**Species:** All variations on a single human genetic template — the original Jensen appears to have been a tall, lean human of Northern European genetic heritage, with angular features and notably pale eyes. Most are fair-skinned, 1.75–1.9m, with angular facial structure and light eyes.

### Ludon — The Meritocracy of Games

**Homeworld:** Ludara — a pleasant temperate world. Its cities are built around large central arenas: the ranked competition venues that are the civic and cultural heart of every Ludaran community.

**Language:** Ludonite names carry Elo rankings embedded within them: 'Kava-Twenty-Three-Forty' indicates a citizen of personal name 'Kava' with an Elo of 2,340. High Elo individuals are addressed before low Elo individuals in any group setting.

**Founding heroes:** The architects of the Elo system — a committee of social theorists and game designers who proposed organizing society around competitive merit rather than inherited status.

**Government:** Led by its highest-Elo citizens. Every citizen participates in weekly ranked competition across a rotating array of physical and mental challenges. The resulting leadership tends to be extraordinarily capable and somewhat lacking in empathy. The GU Navy commander who devised the Apogee mission is Ludonite — her strategic brilliance is not in question. Her concern for the crew she is sending to die is.

**Species:** Standard humans, distinguished by exceptional physical conditioning as a cultural norm. Ludonites tend toward lean, athletic builds and maintain conditioning well into old age.

### Earth — The Rebuilt World and Its Nations

Earth is — was — the homeworld of all humanity and the symbolic heart of the Galactic Union. Now destroyed by the Hyperion Blade, it exists as memory, myth, and wound.

**Notable states:**

- **China** — One of Earth's most continuously existing civilizations. Modern China is remarkably democratic but produces a political monoculture; voters tend toward overwhelming consensus, making Chinese political positions predictable and immovable once formed.

- **The Pacific Coalition** — The island peoples of the Pacific suffered least from the nuclear exchanges. Deeply religious (synthesizing existing Pacific spiritual traditions) and politically communist with significant de-emphasis on technology. The primary political force opposing Replicant GU membership.

- **Great Madagascar** — An imperialist industrialist civilization that spread from Madagascar through Africa and much of the Middle East. Matriarchal: women control government and industry; men fill labor and service roles. Traditional meat consumption is practiced as cultural identity.

- **Mars** — Terraformed in the pre-collapse anchor era. After the Macedonian invasion, Mars has been repurposed as a military-industrial production center — a factory complex producing warships and munitions for a GU counteroffensive that has not yet begun.

---

### Kandia — The Severed World

Kandia is lost. Its anchor was destroyed by Revolutionary forces during the chaos of the Macedonian invasion — a permanent severing that cannot be undone. Before the severance, Kandia was among the GU's most pleasant member states: a lush, temperate world of exceptional natural beauty, and the galaxy's master roboticists. Kandians had automated their entire economy, freeing their population to pursue arts, leisure, and community life.

Kandian expatriates carry a particular grief — the quiet, permanent grief of a door that has closed forever. They tend to be among the most emotionally intelligent and socially aware people one can encounter.

---

### The Ecology Consortium — Guardians of Life

A galaxy-spanning organization dedicated to the preservation of animal biodiversity, management of planetary ecology reserves, and advancement of biology as a science. Their most controversial project is genetic uplifting — modifying animal genomes to approach and eventually reach human-level intelligence. Recent uplift experiments have produced the galaxy's first human-animal genetic hybrids.

The Consortium's most colorful contracted operative is simply known as The Galactic Ass — a mercenary of human-donkey hybrid physiology, currently employed by Macedon to hunt the Apogee. He is, by all accounts, extremely good at his job and entirely aware of how absurd his existence is.

The Ecology Consortium is based in **Gamon** — a GU member state known for its ecological mastery and terraforming expertise, and notably one of the few systems that successfully repelled a Macedonian attack.

---

### The Revolutionaries — The Galaxy Unbound

The Revolutionary movement is not a single faction but an ideology with adherents across dozens of GU member states. Its central belief: that the anchor network is a trap. That humanity will never develop its full potential so long as every civilization is connected to and dependent on every other. That destroying the anchor network — permanently severing every system — would produce a genuine diversification of humanity across thousands of independent worlds.

The GU treats the Revolutionaries as terrorists. The Macedonian War has fractured the movement: some argue for temporary GU alliance against Macedon; others argue for pure non-involvement; a small and dangerous faction argues that Macedon winning might achieve Revolutionary goals by accident.

---

### The Roboticist's Union — The Invisible Power

Arguably the most powerful non-governmental entity in the galaxy. Controlling the manufacture, distribution, and maintenance of robots gives it leverage that most governments find impossible to resist. Its membership spans most major factions; only Macedon is outside the Union's sphere.

The Union's headquarters on Sigg is one of the most heavily secured civilian installations in the galaxy. Its Chairman has held the position for decades, leveraging his connections to maintain the monopoly that makes him powerful.

---

### Luxion Descendants — Heirs to Shame

The Luxion faction no longer exists as a political entity — their homeworld Luxor is a dead world. The survivors were resettled across GU member states as refugees. Two centuries of resettlement have not erased the stigma. Luxion descendants are viewed with suspicion in most GU systems. The majority are simply ordinary citizens. A minority, meeting in secret, harbor the ideology that produced the Star Virus and speak of one day using it again.

---

### Macedonian Dissidents — Truth Speakers in the Dark

Within Macedon, controlled dissent is part of the system. A managed population of apparent rebels exists to be rooted out by loyal citizens. But some dissidents are real — those who have encountered genuine evidence of the Elysium lie. The truth is available; people choose not to believe it.

The most significant dissident the Apogee might encounter is a Macedonian elite — genuinely old, genuinely immortal, genuinely possessed of institutional knowledge accumulated across centuries — who has decided that the oligarchy's project has become genuinely evil and that the only moral action is defection. This individual has no name in public records. They can only be found by those who know where to look.

### D — The Apogee — Ship Summary

- **Class:** GU Stealth-class; unique specification
- **Builder:** Brittlebones (master construction)
- **Propulsion:** State-of-the-art fusion drives; rapid anchor attunement capability
- **Armament:** Not designed for destructive battle; defensive shielding systems
- **Stealth:** Exceptional — small profile plus advanced stealth technology
- **Special systems:** Replicant backup support (re-spawns Replicant crew if body fails); shadow communications relay (provided by Anchormaid dissidents; unreliable but functional)
- **Mission payload:** Fusion bomb — sufficient to destroy the Hyperion Shield
- **Reputation:** Maverick, diverse crew; doesn't always follow orders; always does the right thing

### E — The Hyperion Devices

- **Hyperion Shield:** Moon-sized. Orbits Sol. Blocks 5th-dimensional anchor transit between targeted system pairs. 30-minute window to change blocking frequency. Currently operational.
- **Hyperion Blade:** Moon-sized planet-destroyer. Used to obliterate Earth. Destroyed in the process. Being rebuilt by Macedon.
- Both devices were constructed in Macedonian space — specifically at **Merkaj** — over decades, concealed from GU intelligence.
